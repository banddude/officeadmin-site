What it actually does

Every operational task that used to happen by hand now either runs automatically or gets drafted for review.

Email triage

Watches Gmail, classifies messages as action items, FYI, or noise. Drafts replies in the operator's voice and queues them for approval before sending.

Estimates and invoices

Programmatic access to QuickBooks Online. Creates estimates, invoices, bills, and customer records without opening the app.

Contact management

Direct read/write to Apple Contacts via SQLite. Every customer, vendor, and crew member is an entity with linked tasks, work records, and communication history.

Voice memos

Transcribes recordings with AssemblyAI and identifies speakers using Pinecone voice embeddings. Meeting notes turn into tasks and entity updates automatically.

Scheduling and crews

Integrates with QuickBooks Time for crew scheduling and timesheets. Calendar events sync across Apple Calendar and Google Calendar.

Knowledge graph

MemPalace (open-source) stores 3,580 auto-extracted entities and 6,810 temporal triples from session history. A separate GitHub repo holds 85 manually curated entities for daily operations.

How it works

A Mac Mini at home runs the whole system. Everything else connects to it.

The brain

A Mac Mini running the custom .aiva/ layer on top of open-source tools (OpenClaw, MemPalace, n8n) with Claude as the reasoning engine. It is always on and always connected.

The reach

Phones, laptops, and speakers throughout the house are all participants. Everything syncs via Tailscale VPN and iCloud. AIVA can reach the operator through iMessage, voice, or push notifications.

The memory

A GitHub repo serves as both an operational workspace and a lightweight CRM. Each person and company gets an entity folder with profile data, tasks, and work records.

Meet AIVA

AIVA is the AI agent that runs the system. It is not a chatbot and not a SaaS product. It is an operations system built for a real business.

The core of the system is ~/.aiva/, a custom infrastructure layer. It contains 50+ modules, skills, scripts, MCP servers, and orchestration logic. It sits on top of open-source tools like OpenClaw (terminal UI), MemPalace (knowledge graph), n8n (workflow automation), and BlueBubbles (iMessage bridge), connecting them to Apple frameworks, business APIs, and Claude as the reasoning engine.

AIVA manages background agent sessions in tmux, runs scheduled jobs, responds to iMessages, drafts emails in the operator's voice using a learned rule set, and coordinates everything. It can speak out loud through connected devices, show interactive prompts on the operator's phone, and manage smart home devices through Home Assistant. When it writes something that needs approval, it creates an Apple Reminder. When it learns something new, it stores it in MemPalace. The system is real, running in production, and handles the daily operations of a small business.

Powered by Claude, Python, and a Mac Mini.
