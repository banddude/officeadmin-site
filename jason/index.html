Proposed Plan

One-visit scope that knocks out the network, the loose ends, and the leak detector power in the same trip. Keeps Jason out of ever having to call me back for these three separate things.

1. Network backbone

  • One gateway / router: UniFi Dream Router 7 at the incoming cable/fiber drop (bedroom floor). Handles routing, switching, 6 GHz WiFi 7 in that room, and has a slot for the remaining AP wire.
  • Two ceiling APs on the other levels: one U7 Pro on the main living level, one U7 Pro on the basement/crawlspace-adjacent level. Ceiling-mount, PoE-powered so no outlet needed, invisible once installed.
  • Wiring: one Cat6A run from the bedroom-floor gateway to each AP location, pulled through the existing old-phone-wire chases so we are not opening new wall paths. Old lines come out, Cat6A goes in.

2. Leak detector + future smart-home prep

  • Dedicated circuit + WiFi-reachable outlet for the insurance-required leak detector / shutoff.
  • Confirm the laundry and water-heater zones have coverage too so the leak sensor grid works when we expand.

3. Loose ends from last trip

  • Crawlspace access screen re-secured.
  • Dead phone/telecom lines pulled while we have the walls open.

Why one trip: every wall we open for network runs, the dead phone wires come out of the same wall. The leak-detector circuit drops from the same panel work. The crawlspace screen gets re-done while we are already under the house. Three separate visits becomes one.
